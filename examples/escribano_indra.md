# Will Escribano remain as Indra president?

Indra is Spain's main tech and Defense company (IBEX-35). Shareholders: Spanish State via SEPI (28%), T. Rowe Price (5%), Escribano family via EM&E.

## Key Actors
- Ángel Escribano: President since 2024, co-owner of EM&E, resisted government pressure to resign
- SEPI: State holding, 28% stake, 3 board reps (Miguel Sebastián, Antonio Cuevas, Juan Moscoso), pressuring Escribano out
- José Vicente de los Mozos: CEO since 2023, contract expires June 2026, distanced from Escribano after government approaches
- Spanish Government (Pedro Sánchez): Asked Escribano to resign directly via Manuel de la Rocha, wants control of defense companies
- T. Rowe Price: International fund, raised stake to 5.009%, supports Escribano's continuity
- Independent board members: Divided block, their vote is decisive for any removal
- Belén Gualda: SEPI president, leading the pressure campaign against Escribano

## Timeline (March 2026)
- March 18: SEPI issues statement to CNMV asking Indra to "resolve conflict of interest"
- March 19: EM&E (Escribano's family company) withdraws from integration with Indra to remove the conflict
- March 20-24: Moncloa and SEPI seek support among independent directors, Manuel de la Rocha asks Escribano to resign (refused)
- March 25: Board meeting - SEPI doesn't force vote on Escribano's removal due to uncertain support. Escribano survives.

## Key factors
- Government has 28% via SEPI but lacks board majority to remove Escribano
- International funds (T. Rowe Price increased position) support stability and Escribano
- CEO De los Mozos contract renewal in June 2026 could shift power dynamics
- Shareholder meeting in June 2026 is the next major battleground
- Indra's positive financial results support the current leadership
- European rearmament context favors stability in defense companies
- EM&E withdrew from integration, removing the formal conflict of interest argument
