# Executive Summary

**Confidence:** 85%

The consensus among industry experts and organizations indicates that total software engineering employment is unlikely to decrease in the next five years due to advancements in artificial intelligence (AI). Instead, the integration of AI technologies is expected to create new roles and increase the demand for skilled engineers, reflecting the dynamic nature of the tech industry.

## Key Prediction

Total software engineering employment will remain stable or grow over the next five years as AI advancements create new opportunities rather than eliminate existing jobs.

## Supporting Factors

1. **Creation of New Roles:** The evolution of AI technologies is anticipated to generate new job roles that require skilled software engineers to develop, implement, and manage AI systems.
   
2. **Increased Demand for Skilled Engineers:** As organizations adopt AI technologies, the need for skilled engineers who can work alongside AI tools and systems is expected to rise.

3. **Industry Growth:** The tech industry continues to evolve rapidly, and the integration of AI is seen as a driver of innovation and expansion, further increasing employment opportunities.

4. **Expert Consensus:** A majority of organizations and experts, including major tech companies and regulatory bodies, support the view that AI will enhance rather than diminish job prospects in software engineering.

## Risk Factors

1. **Automation of Routine Tasks:** While AI may automate certain routine coding tasks, the overall impact is expected to lead to job transformation rather than job loss.

2. **Market Volatility:** Economic downturns or shifts in technology adoption could impact employment levels, but the general trend towards AI integration is expected to counteract such risks.

3. **Skill Gaps:** The rapid evolution of technology may create skill gaps that could temporarily affect employment if the workforce does not adapt quickly enough.

## Scenario Analysis

1. **Optimistic Scenario (70% likelihood):** Rapid adoption of AI technologies leads to significant job creation in software engineering, with demand exceeding supply for skilled engineers.

2. **Baseline Scenario (20% likelihood):** Employment levels remain stable with a balanced demand and supply for software engineers, as new roles emerge but some routine tasks are automated.

3. **Pessimistic Scenario (10% likelihood):** Economic downturns or significant shifts in technology adoption lead to a temporary decline in employment levels, but the long-term outlook remains positive as AI integration continues.

## Key Dynamics

- **Integration of AI Technologies:** The ongoing integration of AI into various sectors is expected to create new opportunities for software engineers.
- **Evolving Job Roles:** The nature of software engineering roles will evolve, requiring engineers to adapt and learn new skills to work effectively with AI tools.
- **Industry Collaboration:** Collaboration between tech companies, educational institutions, and regulatory bodies will be crucial in preparing the workforce for the changes brought by AI.

## Timeline

- **Short-term (1-2 years):** Initial integration of AI tools and technologies, with some job transformations occurring but overall employment remaining stable.
- **Medium-term (3-5 years):** Significant growth in new job roles and demand for skilled engineers as AI technologies become more prevalent in the industry.

## Confidence Assessment

The confidence in this prediction is high, supported by a strong consensus among experts, industry leaders, and organizations that view AI as a catalyst for job creation rather than a threat to employment in software engineering.
